1. Overview

In this lab, I built an untethered, open-loop motor control system using the SparkFun RedBoard Artemis Nano and dual H-bridge motor driver boards. The goals were: (1) wire and power the motor drivers safely (current-limited bench supply first), (2) use analogWrite() PWM to regulate motor driver output power, (3) integrate two motors on the car platform, (4) characterize the minimum PWM required for forward motion and on-axis turns on the ground, (5) calibrate left/right motor outputs for straighter driving, and (6) demonstrate an open-loop motion sequence with turns.

Hardware / Software

  • Board: SparkFun RedBoard Artemis Nano
  • Motor driver: dual H-bridge motor driver board (two channels paralleled per motor)
  • Motors: RC car drive motors (left/right)
  • Power: bench supply (debug) + 3.7V LiPo (untethered) for motor VIN; separate supply for Artemis
  • IDE: Arduino IDE
  • Measurement: Tektronix oscilloscope

2. Prelab

2.1 Wiring / Layout Plan

I planned the physical placement of both motor driver boards close to the motors to keep the high-current motor leads short. The Artemis was mounted away from motor power wiring where possible to reduce EMI coupling into control lines. Control signals (PWM/direction) were routed cleanly from Artemis to each driver. Motor VIN and Artemis power were kept on separate supplies (but all grounds are shared).

2.2 Pin Selection

I used four Artemis GPIO/PWM-capable pins to control two motors (two inputs per motor). Each motor driver board uses two paralleled input pins per motor channel (A/B inputs tied together), so each motor is controlled by two Artemis signals: one PWM and one complementary input (0) to set direction.

  • Left motor: PIN_L1, PIN_L2
  • Right motor: PIN_R1, PIN_R2

2.3 Power Strategy (Why Separate Supplies)

The motors draw high transient current (startup / stall), which can cause voltage droop and noise. To avoid brownouts and unstable behavior on the Artemis, I powered the Artemis and motor VIN separately. All grounds (Artemis GND, driver GND, motor battery negative) were tied together to ensure a common reference for PWM control signals.

3. Lab Tasks

3.1 Task 1: Single Motor Driver Bring-up (Bench Supply + Current Limit)

I connected one motor driver board to the Artemis and powered the driver VIN from a current-limited bench supply to reduce risk during debugging. I verified control signals, driver enable, and motor rotation before switching to battery power.

  • Reasonable bench supply settings: VIN set to approximately the LiPo operating range (~3.7–4.2V). I started with a conservative current limit and increased it only as needed to allow motor startup.
  • Common ground: Artemis GND, driver GND, and supply negative were connected.
  • Driver enable: nSLEEP was held high (enabled) during tests.
